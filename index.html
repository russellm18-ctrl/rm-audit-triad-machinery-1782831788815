// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your equipment images—Tigercat skidders, Link-Belt lattice crawlers, T-Mar Log-Champ Swing Yarders—are being indexed as blank files because they carry zero structured alt-text, no JSON-LD schema for LocalBusiness or Product entities, and no EXIF location data tying assets to your six Pacific Northwest branches. Right now, a logging contractor in Eugene or a crane coordinator in Tacoma searching "Link-Belt 228 HSL rental Pacific Northwest" is landing on a competitor whose images are tagged, geolocated, and schema-wrapped. You are invisible at the exact moment of purchase intent.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: Your fleet procurement managers and large-project crane coordinators—the people authorizing six-figure rental contracts for jobs like the $25.8M Cascade Bridge crossing—require visual proof before a phone call happens. You name-drop 50 factory-trained technicians and 25 years of service experience, then show them nothing: no shop floor documentation, no field service crew in action, no machine-condition imagery for your 100-unit rental fleet. A written claim without a photograph is a sales pitch, not evidence, and that audience specifically discounts unverified claims.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: Regional heavy equipment dealers with active Ritchie Bros. and Machinery Trader listings are deploying 360-degree walkaround video, multi-angle condition photography, and hour-meter closeups on every used unit—assets your used sales pages functionally lack. A used Link-Belt excavator buyer comparing listings will default to the dealer whose visual package eliminates uncertainty, and that transaction—typically $180K to $400K per unit—is leaving your lot because your listing gives them nothing to de-risk the decision with. That is not a branding problem; it is a structural conversion failure on high-margin inventory.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Execute a systematic visual asset capture across all six branches: shop technicians mid-repair, rental fleet units staged and documented with condition detail, field service trucks deployed on active Oregon and Washington job sites, and every major product line—Tigercat, Link-Belt, T-Mar—photographed with GPS-embedded EXIF data tied to branch location coordinates. Every image then enters a mandatory metadata deployment protocol: descriptive alt-text referencing machine model, use case, and state; JSON-LD Product and LocalBusiness schema on every equipment and location page; and filename conventions that mirror high-intent search strings your buyers are already using. This is not a photoshoot—it is converting your existing operational activity into a structured, continuously indexed commercial asset that compounds across every search, listing, and sales interaction.
